Prof. Anton Grobler
Title: Responsible technology use and deployment

Biography:
ANTON GROBLER is a qualified industrial psychologist, Master People Practitioner, and an International Affiliate to the Society of Industrial and Organisational Psychology. He holds a Ph.D in Industrial Psychology and is currently the Area Head: Responsible Leadership in Practice at the School of Business Leadership at Unisa. Professor Grober has an extensive academic and institutional research record with numerous publications in accredited peer reviewed academic journals and has delivered papers at various national and international conferences. He is further an NRF rated researcher and serves as the Chief Editor for the African Journal of Employee Relations. His main research focus area is organisational leadership in the African context, specifically its role and contribution to organisational climate, and the impact it has on organisational and employee behaviour.



Avani Singh
Title: Responsible technology use and deployment

Biography:
AVANI SINGH is an attorney of the High Court of South Africa and a certified mediator. Her work focuses on constitutional and human rights law with a specialisation in media freedom, digital rights and emerging technology. Avani consults to a wide range of clients in the public and private sector at all levels of the judicial system, in various parliamentary and law reform processes, as well as before regulatory mechanisms, constitutionally-mandated bodies and commissions of inquiry. At the regional and international levels, she has worked with amongst others the African Union, African Commission on Human and Peoples Rights, Pan-African Parliament, the United Nations Human Rights Committee and the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.



Dr. Stephen Murgatroyd
Title: Leadership in the Digital Era

Biography:
STEPHEN MURGATROYD is a Research Associate, Contact North I Contact Nord since 2005. He was awarded an Honorary Doctorate Athabasca University (2000) for his innovative work in e-learning. Dr Murgatryod is the co-author, with Donald Simpson, of Renaissance Leadership – Rethinking and Leading the Future (2011) and is currently completing a book for Routledge on Futures Leadership for Change (aimed at school leaders). In industry, he has designed, developed, and led leadership development programs for Conoco Phillips. Heinz, Barclays Bank, Tesco, and Oracle; and is currently working with others to develop leadership competency assessment and learning designs for a collective of First Nations (KTC) in Alberta.



Prof. Vincent Maphai
Title: Leading Ethical Technology in a Tsunami: Avoiding Business Dystopia

Biography:
VINCENT MAPHAI has an unusual and distinguished career in academia, private sector, and public service. In academia, he distinguished himself locally and abroad holding fellowships at Harvard, Stanford, and Princeton Universities, and was a visiting Professor at Williams College in Massachusetts. He was a member of various university Councils and is the Chancellor at the Central University of Technology. In business, Vincent was the Director Corporate Affairs and Transformation at The South African Breweries Ltd the Chairman of BHP Billiton, South Africa for 5 years. He is currently the Chairman of the Board of Sibanye-Stilwater, and STADIO Holdings Ltd both companies listed on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange.



Kevin Ramkaloan

Biography:
Since taking up the post of Business Mauritius CEO in 2018, Kevin Ramkaloan has been leading the Mauritian business community in public-private collaborations and partnerships. He has worked closely with local institutions, government organisations and captains of industry. Prior to joining Business Mauritius, Kevin served as Director of the Mauritius Tourism Promotion Authority (MTPA) and, before that, was Director of New Business Development at the Board of Investment (BOI). A former State scholar, Kevin holds postgraduate degrees in economics and policy from the University of Cambridge (UK) and the University of Maine (US).



Prof. Dr Amélé Ekué
Title: Workplace AI and technology – what stays and who goes

Biography:
AMÉLÉ ADAMAVI-AHO EKUÉ, Dr, Professor of ethics, originating from Togo, is serving as academic dean and director of the academic department of Globethics Foundation in Geneva, Switzerland, since 2019. Prior to her appointment to this position she served from 2007 to 2019 as faculty member of the Ecumenical Institute Bossey, attached to the University of Geneva, Switzerland, teaching social ethics, and from 2000 to 2006 as assistant professor at the University of Hamburg, Germany. Her research areas include inter alia intercultural ethics, migration and peace ethics. Amélé has widely published in these fields. Amélé currently serves as visiting professor at higher education institutions in Belgium, Brazil, Germany, and Morocco.



Brad Huddleston

Biography:
BRAD HUDDLESTON is an internationally respected speaker, consultant, teacher, and author on important issues such as technology and culture. He has worked with universities, schools, churches, and law enforcement. He has spoken to hundreds of thousands worldwide on the advantages of well-used technology tools and the dangers of the growing trend toward technology addiction. Brad has an ongoing collaboration with the Bureau of Market Research (BMR)—an independent company—and its Consumer Neuroscience Division at the University of South Africa. Brad has a degree in Computer Science and a Diploma of Biblical Studies. He is a frequent guest on radio and television and the author of Digital Rehab: Learning to Live Again in the Real World., Digital Cocaine: A Journey Toward iBalance, and The Dark Side of Technology: Restoring Balance in the Digital Age. Brad also hosts the international radio show Brad Huddleston’s Techwise. Brad and his wife, Beth, live in the beautiful Shenandoah Valley of Virginia in the United States. You can find out more about Brad at: bradhuddleston.com



Vrigesh Futta

Biography:
VRIGESH FUTTA has spent much of his career across the African continent working with leaders of organizations across mining, finance, manufacturing, retail, and governments in leveraging the potential technology to achieve or exceed top-level priorities. Ex Gartner, he has a BA & MBA in Innovation and leadership. He is now leading the Mauritius Emerging Technologies Council – which has the mandate to promote and support the development and adoption of emerging technologies for the benefit of the Mauritian economy and society.
