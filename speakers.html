Avani Singh
Title: Responsible technology use and deployment
Biography:
Avani Singh is an attorney of the High Court of South Africa and a certified mediator. Her work focuses on constitutional and human rights law with a specialisation in media freedom, digital rights and emerging technology. Avani holds B.Comm. (Law) and LL.B. degrees from the University of Pretoria, and consults to a wide range of clients across organs of state, human rights oversight bodies, civil society, media organisations, online platforms and the private sector across South Africa, the region and further abroad. Avani is the current board chair of Media Monitoring Africa and a council member of the Council for the Advancement of the South African Constitution, and serves as a technical expert to the working group set up to develop guidelines on social media for electoral management bodies.
Avani’s experience brings together litigation, advocacy, research and training, often as multi-pronged strategies that compliment each other. She has practised as an attorney in the Media Law team at Webber Wentzel and the Constitutional Litigation Unit at the Legal Resources Centre, and has clerked at the Constitutional Court of South Africa and the International Criminal Court. Between 2017/2018, Avani co-founded a tech law consultancy and a public interest law firm, ALT Advisory and Power Singh Inc. respectively, which together pioneered a private-public interest law model to widen access to justice for indigent clients.
Domestically, Avani has represented clients at all levels of the judicial system, in various parliamentary and law reform processes, as well as before regulatory mechanisms, constitutionally-mandated bodies and commissions of inquiry. This also integrates with her work at the regional and international levels, including before fora such as the African Union, African Commission on Human and Peoples Rights, Pan-African Parliament, the United Nations Human Rights Committee and the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. In all respects, Avani’s work is driven by an overarching aim towards the meaningful realisation of the right of all persons to receive and impact information and ideas, both on- and offline, by empowering the public to advocate for their rights, make informed decisions and fully participate in the democratic process.


Maxim Jean-Louis
Biography:
Maxim Jean-Louis is President – Chief Executive Officer of Contact North | Contact Nord helping residents in 1,500+ small, rural, remote, Indigenous and Francophone communities, across an area of 1 million square kilometres, access education and training without leaving their communities.
He helped develop Contact North I Contact Nord’s five ground-breaking AI Apps - Tutor Pro: Learn anything, any level, anywhere, anytime; Teaching Assistant Pro: Reduce Your Workload – Enhance Your Teaching; AI Pathfinder Pro: Careers guidance and advice; Online Quick Tips and Skills Tool Kit.
He is a Commonwealth of Learning Fellow and an EDEN Digital Learning Europe (EDEN) Fellow. His current board membership includes Groupe Média TFO; Renewed Computer Technology; Cambrian College; and The Art Gallery of Sudbury. He is a former Vice-Chair of the Board of Directors of the Trillium Foundation; Member of the Board of Governors of Laurentian University; Member of the Government of Ontario Post-Secondary Education Quality Assessment Board; Chair of the Board of Directors of ORION; Member of the Board of Directors of Polar Knowledge; Chair of the Board of the Board of Directors of World University Service of Canada. Prior to arriving at Contact North | Contact Nord in 1996, Maxim Jean-Louis worked in senior management roles for over 15 years at Athabasca University in Alberta, Canada’s Open University.


Dr. Stephen Murgatroyd
Title: Leadership in the Digital Era
Biography:
Stephen Murgatroyd, PhD, is a Research Associate, Contact North I Contact Nord since 2005. He was awarded an Honorary Doctorate Athabasca University (2000) for his innovative work in e-learning. Dr Murgatroyd continues to work in education, for non-profit organizations and the private sector, focusing on leadership for change and transformation, and to publish in academic journals.
He is the founder of the world’s first fully online MBA program in 1994 at Athabasca University; and created the Master’s in Leadership at Middlesex University through Work-Base Learning in 1999-2000. He is currently completing a book for Routledge on Futures Leadership for Change (aimed at school leaders). He teaches (with Dr Deborah Hurst) a module on Developing Leaders (RDLD 680) for Athabasca University’s MBA and EDU596 – Leading Change in the Age of AI at the University of Alberta, and has co-designed of the new MEd degree in Futures Leadership. He is currently developing a Leadership micro-credential for Trent University, Ontario. Dr Murgatryod is the co-author, with Donald Simpson, of Renaissance Leadership – Rethinking and Leading the Future (2011). In industry, he has designed, developed, and led leadership development programs for Conoco Phillips. Heinz, Barclays Bank, Tesco and Oracle; and is currently working with others to develop leadership competency assessment and learning designs for a collective of First Nations (KTC) in Alberta. Stephen has researched and formulated over close to 100 major posts for teachonline.ca. He does the review and authors Must-Read Books on Online Learning and has developed and delivered over 20 training sessions for Contact North I Contact Nord staff and also to staff of Northern Ontario colleges and universities. In higher education specifically, he has published several chapters and key papers.


Prof. Vincent Maphai
Biography:
Vincent Maphai has an unusual and distinguished career in academia, private sector, and public service.
His academic career spans two decades during which he studied at various universities both locally and abroad. He was Professor Extraordinaire in the Department of Political Science at the University of South Africa (UNISA) and holds the same position at the University of Johannesburg. He held fellowships at Harvard University (1988), Princeton University (1989), and Stanford University (1995). From 1991 to 1994 he was Associate Professor and Head of Political Science at the University of the Western Cape (UWC). Until June 2018, when he retired from full-time work, he was a visiting Professor at Williams College in Massachusetts. Dr Maphai served as a Research Executive Director at the Human Sciences Research Council for 3 years. Vincent was also a member of the Council of the University of South Africa and the Chairperson of the Council for the University of KwaZulu-Natal. He is currently the Chancellor at the Central University of Technology.
In the private sector, he served as the Director Corporate Affairs and Transformation at The South African Breweries Ltd for 10 years, and as the Chairman of BHP Billiton, South Africa for 5 years. He is currently the Chairman of the Board of Sibanye-Stilwater, and STADIO Holdings Ltd both companies listed on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange. During his career, he has been involved in various public policy projects and roles including the National Planning Commission, the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC), and Chaired the Presidential Remuneration Review Commission which was tasked with investigating the salaries of public servants. Vincent holds a Ph.D from the University of Natal, obtained in 1991.
