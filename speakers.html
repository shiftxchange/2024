Prof. Anton Grobler
Title: Responsible technology use and deployment
Biography:
ANTON GROBLER is a qualified industrial psychologist, Master People Practitioner, and an International Affiliate to the Society of Industrial and Organisational Psychology. He holds a Ph.D in Industrial Psychology and is currently the Area Head: Responsible Leadership in Practice at the School of Business Leadership at Unisa. Professor Grober has an extensive academic and institutional research record with numerous publications in accredited peer reviewed academic journals and has delivered papers at various national and international conferences. He is further an NRF rated researcher and serves as the Chief Editor for the African Journal of Employee Relations. His main research focus area is organisational leadership in the African context, specifically its role and contribution to organisational climate, and the impact it has on organisational and employee behaviour.


Avani Singh
Title: Responsible technology use and deployment
Biography:
AVANI SINGH is an attorney of the High Court of South Africa and a certified mediator. Her work focuses on constitutional and human rights law with a specialisation in media freedom, digital rights and emerging technology. Avani consults to a wide range of clients in the public and private sector at all levels of the judicial system, in various parliamentary and law reform processes, as well as before regulatory mechanisms, constitutionally-mandated bodies and commissions of inquiry. At the regional and international levels, she has worked with amongst others the African Union, African Commission on Human and Peoples Rights, Pan-African Parliament, the United Nations Human Rights Committee and the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Prof. Vincent Maphai
Biography:
VINCENT MAPHAI has an unusual and distinguished career in academia, private sector, and public service. In academia, he distinguished himself locally and abroad holding fellowships at Harvard, Stanford, and Princeton Universities, and was a visiting Professor at Williams College in Massachusetts. He was a member of various university Councils and is the Chancellor at the Central University of Technology. In business, Vincent was the Director Corporate Affairs and Transformation at The South African Breweries Ltd the Chairman of BHP Billiton, South Africa for 5 years. He is currently the Chairman of the Board of Sibanye-Stilwater, and STADIO Holdings Ltd both companies listed on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange.
